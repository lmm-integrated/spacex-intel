## What this is

A consolidated, sourced read on the current state of SpaceX's business. The headline reframe: **SpaceX is no longer just the rocket company.** After absorbing xAI in February 2026 and IPO'ing on Nasdaq in June 2026, it is a consolidated space + connectivity + AI conglomerate. Most of the confusing "deals" in the news collapse onto one corporate parent.

This brief was assembled from primary filings (SpaceX's SEC Form S-1 / S-1/A, official @SpaceX and Nvidia posts, Anthropic's own announcements), top-tier press, and a validation pass against X.com. Every section carries sources; see [SOURCES.md](SOURCES.md) for the master list.

## How to read it

- **Confidence tags:** `[HIGH]` = confirmed against a primary source or multiple independent outlets. `[MED]` = solid reporting, some variance across sources. `[LOW]` = single-source, estimate, or computed by us.
- **"Announced vs installed":** Musk/marketing figures consistently run ahead of physically operational capacity. Where they diverge, both are given.
- **"Raw vs H100-equivalent":** GPU counts are stated as raw chips unless explicitly normalized to H100-equivalent compute.
- Dollar totals on the compute leases are headline/run-rate figures; most carry 90-day mutual cancellation clauses, so treat multi-year totals as soft.

## The one-paragraph version

In ~5 months Musk turned SpaceX into a vertically integrated space-and-AI giant. SpaceX absorbed xAI (which already owned X and Grok) on Feb 2, 2026 in a ~$1.25T all-stock merger, then IPO'd on Nasdaq as **SPCX** on June 12, 2026 at $135/share (~$75B raised, the largest IPO ever), made Musk the first trillionaire, agreed to buy Cursor-maker Anysphere for $60B, and is funding a cash-burning AI arm via the IPO, a debut investment-grade bond (launched June 22), and multi-billion-dollar compute leases to its own rivals Anthropic, Google, and Reflection AI. The AI segment lost ~$6.4B in 2025. The next bet is putting data centers in orbit (the AI1 satellite). For where the pieces could go and whether the ~$2.2T valuation holds up, see [08-forward-thesis-valuation.md](08-forward-thesis-valuation.md).

# 10 · Catalyst calendar (tracker)

Quick-reference timeline of dated events that confirm or break the thesis. Point-in-time as of 2026-06-22; dates marked **(soft)** are targets that slip. Impact = how much it moves the thesis. Signal = what a bull vs bear outcome looks like.

## The 3 that matter most

1. **Dec 31, 2026 -> Q1 2027: the 90-day lease windows open.** Anthropic and Google can cancel after this. Renew = the ~$26-38B compute line is durable (huge bull). Cancel = the largest revenue driver evaporates and xAI's ~$10B/yr burn gets dangerous. **This is the single biggest swing factor.**
2. **~Late July to August 2026: first post-IPO 10-Q (Q2).** First audited reality check. Watch the **Commitments & Contingencies footnote** for any firm Vera Rubin / GPU purchase commitment, plus Q2 capex/burn and whether compute-lease revenue is ramping as billed.
3. **Starship return to flight (Flight 13).** Gated on the FAA closing the Flight 12 mishap. Unlocks operational Starlink V3 and de-risks AI1. No flight = the whole Starlink-growth + space-AI narrative stalls.

## How to read it as a tracker

- **Through Dec 2026** the calendar is mostly *supply* (lockup unlocks) plus *execution proof points* (Starship, Grok 5, 10-Qs). Net: choppy, with a known supply overhang building Aug-Dec.
- **The hinge is the turn of 2027:** the lease windows (Dec 31) and the Q1 renew-or-cancel decision determine whether the largest bull revenue driver is real. Everything in [09-bull-case-what-if.md](09-bull-case-what-if.md) ultimately rides on that plus Starship.
- A disciplined watch order: bond pricing -> Q2 10-Q -> Flight 13 -> Aug 11 unlock -> Grok 5 -> Q3 10-Q/Nov tranche -> **Dec 31 lease windows / Q1 2027 renewals** -> June 2027 Musk unlock.
